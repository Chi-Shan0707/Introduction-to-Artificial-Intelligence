1. Support Vector Machines (SVM) 1. 支持向量机 (SVM)

Support Vector Machines (SVM) represent a landmark in the history of machine learning. The core intuition is to find a decision boundary (hyperplane) that not only separates the data correctly but also maintains the maximum possible margin from the nearest data points of any class. This margin acts as a "safety buffer," enhancing the model's ability to generalize to unseen data.

Key Concept 核心概念: Support Vectors

The decision is determined solely by the points closest to the boundary, known as Support Vectors. Points further away do not affect the position of the hyperplane. This sparsity property makes SVM both memory-efficient and robust.

"The goal of SVM is not merely to separate, but to separate with maximum confidence."

2. Hard-Margin SVM 2. 硬间隔 SVM

In a perfectly linearly separable dataset, we seek a hyperplane that correctly classifies every point while maximizing the geometric margin. This section traces the full derivation from motivation to final QP formulation.

2.1 Problem Motivation & First Attempt

We want to find a hyperplane

这个目标函数同时体现两层含义： 1. 几何意义：最小化 $\|w\|$ 等价于最大化几何间隔。 2. 统计学习意义： $$ \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 $$ 就是标准 $L_2$ 正则项（Ridge/Weight Decay 形式）。
$$w^T x + b = 0$$

that not only correctly classifies all samples but also keeps the boundary "as safe as possible" — i.e., as far from the nearest points of both classes as possible.

The most intuitive idea is to maximize \( |w^T x + b| \). However, this fails for three reasons:

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  1. The absolute value only reflects distance, not direction.
  2. A misclassified sample far from the plane can have a large \( |w^T x + b| \).
    3. 根据课堂 PPT 的推导语境，临时变量 $$ \beta = |w^T x + b| $$ 确实被用作“点到超平面的未归一化绝对距离”描述量。这个定义本身没有问题，但它是无方向标量，不能体现分类是否正确，因此不能直接作为分类学习的最终优化依据。
  3. Therefore, "maximizing absolute distance" would treat misclassified points as "good" samples — the optimization objective contradicts the classification goal.
Key Insight: We need a quantity that encodes both correctness and confidence simultaneously. The absolute value \( \beta = |w^T x + b| \) loses label information and cannot distinguish correct from incorrect classifications.

2.2 Functional Margin

Introducing binary labels \( y_i \in \{-1, +1\} \), we define the functional margin of sample \( (x_i, y_i) \) as:

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$f_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b)$$

This definition binds "correctness" and "confidence" together:

  • Positive sample correctly classified: \( y_i = +1 \) and \( w^T x_i + b > 0 \), so \( f_i > 0 \).
    • 引入二分类标签 $$ y_i \in \{-1, +1\} $$ 定义函数间隔 $$ f_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b) $$
  • Negative sample correctly classified: \( y_i = -1 \) and \( w^T x_i + b < 0 \), so \( f_i > 0 \) (negative × negative = positive).
    • 引入二分类标签 $$ y_i \in \{-1, +1\} $$ 定义函数间隔 $$ f_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b) $$
  • Any misclassification yields \( f_i < 0 \).
    • 引入二分类标签 $$ y_i \in \{-1, +1\} $$ 定义函数间隔 $$ f_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b) $$

Thus, the sign of \( f_i \) determines correctness, and the magnitude measures confidence.

这就引出下一步：引入松弛变量 $\xi_i$，从硬约束走向 Soft-Margin SVM。
Why "functional"? The functional margin depends on the functional form of \( (w, b) \) — it can be arbitrarily scaled without changing the geometric decision boundary. This is both a feature and a limitation.

2.3 Geometric Margin

The functional margin suffers from a scaling problem: if we multiply \( (w, b) \) by a constant \( c > 0 \), the hyperplane stays the same but \( f_i \) is multiplied by \( c \). To fix this, we normalize by \( \|w\| \) to obtain the geometric margin:

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$\gamma_i = \frac{y_i(w^T x_i + b)}{\|w\|} = \frac{f_i}{\|w\|}$$

The geometric margin is invariant to uniform scaling of \( (w, b) \) because the numerator and denominator scale by the same factor. It represents the actual Euclidean distance from the point to the hyperplane.

$$\text{Geometric margin} = \frac{\text{Functional margin}}{\|w\|}$$

2.4 Max-Min Modeling: The Bucket Effect

For a fixed hyperplane \( (w, b) \), the overall classification confidence is determined by the weakest link — the point with the smallest functional margin:

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$F = \min_i f_i = \min_i y_i(w^T x_i + b)$$

This naturally leads to the maximin (worst-case optimal) objective — the "bucket effect" where the shortest stave determines the bucket's capacity:

$$\max_{w,b}\, \min_i\, y_i(w^T x_i + b)$$

This is the core SVM intuition: maximize the margin of the closest point.

这就引出下一步：引入松弛变量 $\xi_i$，从硬约束走向 Soft-Margin SVM。

2.5 Scale Crisis

If we scale \( (w, b) \) to \( (cw, cb) \) for any \( c > 0 \):

若把 $(w,b)$ 同比例放大为 $(cw,cb)$，$c>0$： 1. 超平面几何位置不变（同一个决策边界）。 2. 但函数间隔 $f_i$ 会被放大为 $cf_i$。
  • The geometric position of the hyperplane does not change (same decision boundary).
  • But the functional margin \( f_i \) is scaled to \( c \cdot f_i \).
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

For example, \( w_1 = (3, 4) \) and \( w_2 = (30, 40) \) represent the same normal direction, but the functional margin is inflated 10×. This causes the objective

$$\max F \to \infty$$

by infinitely scaling the parameters, making the problem ill-posed with no unique meaningful solution.

The Scale Crisis: Without normalization, the maximization problem is unbounded — any objective value can be achieved by simply scaling up \( w \) and \( b \). We must eliminate this redundancy.

2.6 Geometric Margin Normalization

To eliminate the artificial scaling, we use the geometric margin for optimization:

$$\max_{w,b} M \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \gamma_i \ge M, \quad i = 1, \dots, m$$

The geometric margin is scale-invariant because dividing by \( \|w\| \) cancels any uniform scaling of \( (w, b) \).

2.7 Anchor Point Normalization — Why the Constant 1?

We exploit the remaining scaling freedom to impose an anchor normalization:

$$\min_i\, y_i(w^T x_i + b) = 1$$

This means: "the support vectors (closest points) have a functional margin of exactly 1." Under this normalization, the minimum geometric margin becomes:

$$M = \frac{1}{\|w\|}$$

The constant 1 is not arbitrary — it serves to eliminate the equivalence class \( \{(cw, cb) \mid c > 0\} \) of redundant parameters. Consider:

不等式右侧常数 1 的意义就在这里：它不是随意设定，而是用于消除参数等价类 $$ \{(cw,cb)\mid c>0\} $$ 带来的冗余自由度。
  • \( 2x + 3y \ge 1 \) and \( 20x + 30y \ge 10 \) are equivalent constraints (same geometric boundary).
  • But forcing the right-hand side to be 1 means \( 20x + 30y \ge 1 \) is now a different (stricter) constraint — the artificial inflation has been removed.
    • 对应的无约束视角可写成 Hinge Loss + $L_2$ 正则化： $$ \min_{w,b} \sum_{i=1}^m \max\left(0,1-y_i(w^T x_i+b)\right) + \lambda\|w\|^2 $$ 其中 $C$ 与 $\lambda$ 体现同一组偏好，方向上呈反向关系。
Summary: The constant 1 is a normalization convention that fixes the scale ambiguity. It is equivalent to choosing units where the support vector's functional margin equals 1, making all distances measurable in these units.

2.8 Primal QP Formula 公式tion

With the anchor normalization, the Hard-Margin SVM becomes:

线性可分支持向量机（Hard-Margin SVM）统一笔记
$$\max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1, \; \forall i$$

Equivalently (and more commonly written as a minimization for computational convenience):

$$\min_{w,b} \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1, \; \forall i$$
Why \( \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 \) instead of \( \|w\| \)?
  1. \( \|w\|^2 \) is a smooth quadratic function — optimization is more stable.
  2. The \( \frac{1}{2} \) cancels the factor 2 from differentiation, giving cleaner gradients: \( \nabla_w \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 = w \).
  3. This form is a standard Convex Quadratic Program (QP) with linear inequality constraints, enabling the use of mature QP solvers.

It is an equivalent reformulation for computational friendliness, not a change to the problem.

This is a Convex Quadratic Programming (QP) problem — every local optimum is a global optimum, guaranteeing a unique solution (when the feasible region is non-empty).

2.9 L2 Regularization Interpretation

The objective function carries a dual interpretation:

  1. Geometric meaning: Minimizing \( \|w\| \) maximizes the geometric margin — the "widest street" between classes.
  2. Statistical learning meaning: \( \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 \) is the standard \( L_2 \) regularization term (Ridge / Weight Decay).
    3. 这个目标函数同时体现两层含义： 1. 几何意义：最小化 $\|w\|$ 等价于最大化几何间隔。 2. 统计学习意义： $$ \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 $$ 就是标准 $L_2$ 正则项（Ridge/Weight Decay 形式）。

In the hard-margin setting:

线性可分支持向量机（Hard-Margin SVM）统一笔记
  • The constraints \( y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1 \) guarantee zero training error (empirical risk = 0).
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  • The objective minimizes parameter norm, controlling model complexity (related to VC dimension or Rademacher complexity).
  • This corresponds to Structural Risk Minimization (SRM).
"SVM appears to do geometric optimization (widest street), but underneath it performs constrained \( L_2 \) regularization for complexity control."

Is the \( \|w\| \) in the denominator regularization?

Strictly speaking, the \( \|w\| \) in the geometric margin formula is not directly a regularization term — it is geometric normalization. However, after normalization, maximizing the geometric margin is equivalent to minimizing \( \|w\|^2 \), which is regularization. In short: it is not regularization itself, but it leads to regularization.

3. Soft-Margin SVM 3. 软间隔 SVM

Real-world data is rarely perfectly separable due to noise, outliers, and class overlap. The hard-margin assumption may yield no feasible solution, or force the boundary to accommodate anomalous points at the cost of overall margin — leading to overfitting.

3.1 Slack Variables

Soft-Margin SVM introduces slack variables \( \xi_i \ge 0 \) to relax the hard constraints:

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1 - \xi_i$$

The slack variables have a clear geometric interpretation:

  • \( \xi_i = 0 \): The sample is on or outside the margin boundary — correctly classified with full margin.
  • \( 0 < \xi_i < 1 \): The sample is correctly classified but falls inside the margin.
  • \( \xi_i \ge 1 \): The sample is misclassified.

3.2 Penalty Coefficient C

The primal objective of Soft-Margin SVM is:

这就引出下一步：引入松弛变量 $\xi_i$，从硬约束走向 Soft-Margin SVM。
$$\min_{w,b,\xi} \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 + C \sum_{i=1}^m \xi_i$$ $$\text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1 - \xi_i, \quad \xi_i \ge 0, \; \forall i$$

The parameter \( C \) controls the trade-off between margin width and classification error:

  • Large \( C \): Heavily penalizes misclassifications → strict, narrow margin → risk of overfitting.
  • Small \( C \): Tolerates more errors → wide, flexible margin → risk of underfitting.
When \( C \to \infty \): The soft-margin SVM reduces to the hard-margin case, as any violation becomes infinitely costly. When \( C \to 0 \), the model ignores all data and maximizes margin freely.

3.3 Hinge Loss Formula 公式tion

The soft-margin objective can be equivalently rewritten as an unconstrained loss minimization problem:

$$\min_{w,b} \sum_{i=1}^m \max\left(0,\; 1 - y_i(w^T x_i + b)\right) + \lambda \|w\|^2$$

where:

  • \( \max(0, 1 - z) \) is the Hinge Loss, which penalizes predictions that are either wrong or too close to the boundary.
  • \( \lambda = 1/C \) is the regularization strength (inverse of the penalty coefficient).
$$\text{Hinge Loss}(z) = \max(0, 1 - z) = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } z \ge 1 \\ 1 - z & \text{if } z < 1 \end{cases}$$
Connection to Regularization: The Hinge Loss formulation makes explicit that SVM is a member of the "loss + regularization" family. The loss measures empirical fit; the \( L_2 \) term controls complexity. The parameter \( C \) (or equivalently \( \lambda \)) balances the two.

4. Dual Formulation & Kernel Trick 4. 对偶问题与核技巧

To handle non-linear boundaries and exploit high-dimensional feature spaces, we reformulate SVM in its dual form using Lagrange multipliers. This dual perspective naturally leads to the kernel trick.

4.1 Lagrangian Derivation

We construct the Lagrangian for the hard-margin primal problem by introducing multipliers \( \alpha_i \ge 0 \):

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$\mathcal{L}(w, b, \alpha) = \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 - \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i \left[ y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1 \right]$$

Taking partial derivatives and setting them to zero (stationarity conditions from KKT):

With respect to \( w \):

根据课堂 PPT 的推导语境，临时变量 $$ \beta = |w^T x + b| $$ 确实被用作“点到超平面的未归一化绝对距离”描述量。这个定义本身没有问题，但它是无方向标量，不能体现分类是否正确，因此不能直接作为分类学习的最终优化依据。
$$\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial w} = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; w = \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i y_i x_i$$

This reveals that the optimal weight vector is a linear combination of the training samples, weighted by \( \alpha_i y_i \).

这个目标函数同时体现两层含义： 1. 几何意义：最小化 $\|w\|$ 等价于最大化几何间隔。 2. 统计学习意义： $$ \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 $$ 就是标准 $L_2$ 正则项（Ridge/Weight Decay 形式）。

With respect to \( b \):

根据课堂 PPT 的推导语境，临时变量 $$ \beta = |w^T x + b| $$ 确实被用作“点到超平面的未归一化绝对距离”描述量。这个定义本身没有问题，但它是无方向标量，不能体现分类是否正确，因此不能直接作为分类学习的最终优化依据。
$$\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial b} = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i y_i = 0$$

4.2 KKT Conditions

The Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions provide necessary and sufficient conditions for optimality (since the problem is convex):

  1. Stationarity: \( \nabla_w \mathcal{L} = 0 \), \( \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial b} = 0 \)
    2. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  2. Primal feasibility: \( y_i(w^T x_i + b) \ge 1, \; \forall i \)
    3. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  3. Dual feasibility: \( \alpha_i \ge 0, \; \forall i \)
    4. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  4. Complementary slackness: \( \alpha_i [y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1] = 0, \; \forall i \)
    5. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

Complementary Slackness — Who are the Support Vectors?

The complementary slackness condition implies:

  • If \( \alpha_i > 0 \), then \( y_i(w^T x_i + b) = 1 \) — the point lies exactly on the margin boundary. These are the support vectors.
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  • If \( y_i(w^T x_i + b) > 1 \) (point is safely inside its class), then \( \alpha_i = 0 \) — the point does not influence the decision boundary.
    • 引入二分类标签 $$ y_i \in \{-1, +1\} $$ 定义函数间隔 $$ f_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b) $$

This sparsity property is fundamental: the solution depends only on a subset of training points.

4.3 The Dual Optimization Problem

Substituting the stationarity conditions back into the Lagrangian yields the dual problem:

$$\max_{\alpha} \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i - \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^n \alpha_i \alpha_j y_i y_j (x_i \cdot x_j)$$ $$\text{s.t.} \quad 0 \le \alpha_i \le C, \quad \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i y_i = 0$$

Key observations:

  • The dual depends on the data only through dot products \( x_i \cdot x_j \).
    • 最大化最小几何间隔可写为 $$ \max_{w,b} M \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \gamma_i \ge M,\ i=1,\dots,m $$
  • The constraint \( \alpha_i \le C \) comes from the soft-margin formulation; for hard-margin, \( C = \infty \).
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  • This is also a QP, but in \( n \) variables (one per sample) rather than \( d \) variables (one per feature). When \( n \ll d \), the dual is much more efficient.
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

4.4 The Kernel Trick

Since the dual problem depends only on dot products \( x_i \cdot x_j \), we can replace the dot product with a kernel function:

$$K(x_i, x_j) = \phi(x_i)^T \phi(x_j)$$

This implicitly maps the data into a high-dimensional (possibly infinite-dimensional) feature space via \( \phi(\cdot) \), without ever computing \( \phi(x) \) explicitly. The kernel computes the inner product directly in the original space.

The decision function becomes:

$$f(x) = \text{sign}\left( \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i y_i K(x_i, x) + b \right)$$
The Power of the Kernel Trick: We can operate in an exponentially large (or infinite) feature space while paying only the cost of a kernel evaluation in the original space. This is what allows SVM to learn non-linear decision boundaries.

4.5 Kernel Types

Kernel Type Formula Implicit Feature Space Use Case
Linear \( K(x, z) = x^T z \) Original space (no mapping) Linearly separable data, high-dimensional text features, when \( n \ll d \)
Polynomial \( K(x, z) = (x^T z + c)^d \) All monomials up to degree \( d \) Captures feature interactions; image/text with moderate nonlinearity
RBF (Gaussian) \( K(x, z) = \exp(-\gamma \|x - z\|^2) \) Infinite-dimensional Most popular default; captures complex nonlinear manifolds
Sigmoid \( K(x, z) = \tanh(\alpha x^T z + c) \) Similar to neural network activation Connection to neural networks; less commonly used in practice
How to choose a kernel?
  • Start with the linear kernel — it is fast and often sufficient.
  • If the data is clearly non-linear, try the RBF kernel (default in most libraries).
  • The RBF kernel has one hyperparameter \( \gamma \): large \( \gamma \) → narrow, sharp boundaries (risk of overfitting); small \( \gamma \) → smooth boundaries (risk of underfitting).
  • Use cross-validation to tune \( \gamma \) and \( C \) jointly.
    • 为消除尺度虚增，引入几何间隔： $$ \gamma_i = \frac{y_i(w^T x_i+b)}{\|w\|} = \frac{f_i}{\|w\|} $$

4.6 Mercer's Theorem

Not every function is a valid kernel. Mercer's Theorem provides the necessary and sufficient condition:

Mercer's Theorem

A function \( K(x, z) \) is a valid kernel (i.e., there exists a feature map \( \phi \) such that \( K(x, z) = \phi(x)^T \phi(z) \)) if and only if:

当低维空间线性不可分时，可考虑映射 $$ x \mapsto \phi(x) $$ 在高维特征空间做线性分类。若显式构造 $\phi(x)$ 成本很高，可用核技巧： $$ K(x,z)=\phi(x)^T\phi(z) $$ 直接在原空间计算内积，等价完成“隐式升维”。
  1. Symmetry: \( K(x, z) = K(z, x) \) for all \( x, z \).经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
    2. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  2. Positive Semi-Definiteness (PSD): For any finite set of points \( \{x_1, \dots, x_n\} \), the Gram matrix \( G_{ij} = K(x_i, x_j) \) is positive semi-definite (i.e., \( v^T G v \ge 0 \) for all vectors \( v \)).
    3. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

This theorem is powerful because it allows us to design kernels by checking algebraic properties, without ever constructing \( \phi \) explicitly. It also enables combining kernels (sum, product, scaling) while preserving validity.

5. Model Evaluation Strategies 5. 模型评估策略

How do we know if our model truly "learned" or just "memorized"? We must evaluate it on data it has never seen during training. This section covers the fundamental strategies for reliable performance estimation.

5.1 Dataset Splitting

A well-designed evaluation requires partitioning the available data into distinct roles:

  • Training Set: Used to learn model parameters (weights, support vectors, etc.). The model directly "sees" this data.
  • Validation Set: Used to tune hyperparameters (e.g., \( C \), \( \gamma \), kernel type) and perform model selection. The model does not train on this data, but the developer uses it to make decisions.
  • Testing Set: The "Final Exam." Strictly isolated until the very end to provide an unbiased estimate of generalization error. Must never influence any design decision.
Data Leakage: If any information from the test set leaks into the training or model selection process (e.g., using test accuracy to pick hyperparameters), the reported performance will be optimistically biased and unreliable.

5.2 Hold-out Method

The simplest approach: randomly split the data once, e.g., 80% training / 20% testing (or 70/15/15 for train/val/test).

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

Advantages: Simple, fast, one-time cost.

Disadvantages:

  • High variance: the performance estimate depends heavily on which points end up in which split.
  • Wastes data: a significant portion is never used for training.
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  • Unreliable for small datasets.
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$

5.3 K-Fold Cross-Validation in Detail

K-fold cross-validation provides a more robust estimate by using every data point for both training and validation. The procedure:

  1. Shuffle the dataset randomly (with a fixed seed for reproducibility).
    2. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  2. Split the data into \( K \) equal-sized (or approximately equal) folds: \( D_1, D_2, \ldots, D_K \).
  3. For each fold \( i = 1, 2, \ldots, K \):
    • Use \( D_i \) as the validation/test set.
      • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
    • Use the union of all other folds \( D_{-i} = D_1 \cup \cdots \cup D_{i-1} \cup D_{i+1} \cup \cdots \cup D_K \) as the training set.
      • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
    • Train the model on \( D_{-i} \) and evaluate on \( D_i \) to obtain \( \text{score}_i \).
      • 最大化最小几何间隔可写为 $$ \max_{w,b} M \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \gamma_i \ge M,\ i=1,\dots,m $$
  4. Compute the mean and standard deviation of the \( K \) scores.
$$\bar{S} = \frac{1}{K}\sum_{i=1}^{K} \text{score}_i, \qquad \sigma_S = \sqrt{\frac{1}{K-1}\sum_{i=1}^{K}(\text{score}_i - \bar{S})^2}$$

Common choices: \( K = 5 \) (faster, slightly higher bias) or \( K = 10 \) (standard, good bias-variance trade-off for moderate dataset sizes).

Bias-Variance Trade-off of \( K \):
  • Small \( K \) (e.g., \( K = 2 \)): Each fold uses only half the data for training → high bias (model underfits due to insufficient data).
  • Large \( K \) (e.g., \( K = N \), LOOCV): Nearly all data used for training each fold → low bias, but the \( N \) fold estimates are highly correlated → high variance in the final estimate.
    • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  • \( K = 5 \) or \( K = 10 \): Typically offers the best compromise between bias and variance of the performance estimate.

5.4 Stratified K-Fold Cross-Validation

When the dataset has class imbalance (e.g., 95% negative, 5% positive), a random split may produce folds where some folds contain very few or zero positive samples. This makes the performance estimate unreliable.

Stratified K-Fold addresses this by ensuring each fold preserves the same class distribution as the original dataset:

  1. Separate the data by class: group all positive samples and all negative samples.
  2. Within each class, divide the samples into \( K \) roughly equal groups.
  3. For fold \( i \), combine the \( i \)-th group from the positive class with the \( i \)-th group from the negative class.
    4. 这时原始问题写为： $$ \min_{w,b,\xi} \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 + C\sum_{i=1}^m \xi_i $$ $$ \mathrm{s.t.}\quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1-\xi_i,\ \xi_i\ge 0,\ \forall i $$
  4. Each fold now has approximately the same positive-to-negative ratio as the full dataset.
When to Use: Always prefer stratified K-fold for classification tasks with imbalanced classes. Standard K-fold may yield folds with no positive samples at all, making evaluation meaningless.

5.5 Nested Cross-Validation for Hyperparameter Tuning

If we tune hyperparameters using standard K-fold CV and then report the best CV score as our performance estimate, we are overfitting to the validation set — the estimate is optimistically biased. Nested cross-validation solves this by separating the concerns of model selection and performance estimation.

Procedure (Nested \( K_1 \times K_2 \) CV):

当低维空间线性不可分时，可考虑映射 $$ x \mapsto \phi(x) $$ 在高维特征空间做线性分类。若显式构造 $\phi(x)$ 成本很高，可用核技巧： $$ K(x,z)=\phi(x)^T\phi(z) $$ 直接在原空间计算内积，等价完成“隐式升维”。
  1. Outer Loop (\( K_1 \) folds): Split data into \( K_1 \) outer folds. For each outer fold \( i \):
    • Hold out the outer test fold \( D_i^{\text{outer}} \).
      • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
    • Use the remaining data \( D_{-i}^{\text{outer}} \) for the inner loop.
      • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  2. Inner Loop (\( K_2 \) folds on \( D_{-i}^{\text{outer}} \)): For each candidate hyperparameter setting \( \theta_j \):
    • Perform \( K_2 \)-fold CV on \( D_{-i}^{\text{outer}} \) to obtain mean score \( \bar{S}(\theta_j) \).
      • 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  3. Select the best hyperparameters: \( \theta^* = \arg\max_{\theta_j} \bar{S}(\theta_j) \).
    4. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
  4. Evaluate the model trained with \( \theta^* \) on \( D_i^{\text{outer}} \) to get \( \text{score}_i^{\text{outer}} \).
    5. 最大化最小几何间隔可写为 $$ \max_{w,b} M \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \gamma_i \ge M,\ i=1,\dots,m $$
  5. Repeat for all \( K_1 \) outer folds, then report: \( \bar{S}^{\text{outer}} = \frac{1}{K_1}\sum_{i=1}^{K_1} \text{score}_i^{\text{outer}} \).
    6. 经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$\text{Total model fits} = K_1 \times K_2 \times |\{\theta_j\}|$$
Why Nested CV Matters: The outer loop provides an unbiased estimate of generalization error because the outer test data was never used to select hyperparameters. Without nesting, reported performance is often inflated. This is critical when comparing multiple models or publishing results.

6. Performance Metrics 6. 评估指标

Accuracy is often misleading in imbalanced datasets (e.g., fraud detection where 99.9% of transactions are normal — a model that always predicts "normal" achieves 99.9% accuracy but catches zero fraud). We need more nuanced metrics.

6.1 Confusion Matrix 混淆矩阵

The confusion matrix is the foundation of all classification metrics:

Predicted Positive Predicted Negative
Actual Positive True Positive (\( TP \)) False Negative (\( FN \)) — Type II Error
Actual Negative False Positive (\( FP \)) — Type I Error True Negative (\( TN \))

6.2 Precision, Recall, and F1-Score F1分数

$$\text{Precision} = \frac{TP}{TP + FP} \qquad \text{(Of all predicted positives, how many are correct?)}$$ $$\text{Recall (Sensitivity / TPR)} = \frac{TP}{TP + FN} \qquad \text{(Of all actual positives, how many did we find?)}$$

Precision and recall are often in tension — improving one may hurt the other. The F1-Score is their harmonic mean:

$$\text{F1-Score} = \frac{2 \cdot \text{Precision} \cdot \text{Recall}}{\text{Precision} + \text{Recall}}$$

The harmonic mean ensures that F1 is high only when both precision and recall are high. Additional metrics:

$$\text{Specificity (TNR)} = \frac{TN}{TN + FP}, \qquad \text{FPR} = \frac{FP}{FP + TN} = 1 - \text{Specificity}$$

6.3 ROC and PR Curve PR曲线s

By varying the classification threshold, we trace out curves that reveal performance across all operating points:

  • ROC Curve (Receiver Operating Characteristic): Plots TPR (Recall) vs. FPR at different thresholds. The AUC-ROC (Area Under the ROC Curve) represents the probability that a randomly chosen positive sample is ranked higher than a randomly chosen negative sample. AUC = 1.0 is perfect; AUC = 0.5 is random.
  • PR Curve (Precision-Recall): Plots Precision vs. Recall at different thresholds. Preferred for highly imbalanced datasets because ROC can be overly optimistic when negatives dominate.
When to use which?
  • Use ROC-AUC when classes are roughly balanced and you care about both positive and negative performance.
  • Use PR curves when the positive class is rare (e.g., disease detection, fraud detection, anomaly detection).

6.4 Average Precision (AP) and mAP

6.4.1 Definition 定义 of AP

Average Precision (AP) is defined as the area under the Precision-Recall (PR) curve. It provides a single scalar value that summarizes the precision-recall trade-off across all possible classification thresholds:

$$\text{AP} = \int_0^1 P(r) \, dr$$

where \( P(r) \) is the precision measured at recall level \( r \).

6. 最终原始问题（Primal）

6.4.2 Interpolation Method

In practice, the PR curve is not smooth — precision can fluctuate as recall increases. To obtain a monotonically decreasing curve, we apply interpolation: at each recall level \( r \), the interpolated precision is the maximum precision observed at any recall level \( \tilde{r} \ge r \):

$$P_{\text{interp}}(r) = \max_{\tilde{r} \ge r} P(\tilde{r})$$

This eliminates the "zigzag" behavior of the raw PR curve, producing a monotonically decreasing envelope that yields a more stable and meaningful metric.

6.4.3 Mathematical Formula 公式tion (All-Point)

The discrete approximation of AP is a Riemann sum over observed recall points:

$$\text{AP} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} P(k) \cdot \Delta r(k)$$

where:

  • \( P(k) \) is the precision at cutoff \( k \) (among the top-\( k \) ranked predictions),
  • \( \Delta r(k) = r(k) - r(k-1) \) is the change in recall from cutoff \( k-1 \) to \( k \),
  • \( n \) is the total number of predictions.

6.4.4 11-Point Interpolation

The classic PASCAL VOC challenge uses an 11-point interpolation method, sampling precision at 11 equally spaced recall points: \( r \in \{0.0, 0.1, 0.2, \ldots, 1.0\} \):

$$\text{AP}_{11} = \frac{1}{11} \sum_{r \in \{0.0,\,0.1,\,\ldots,\,1.0\}} P_{\text{interp}}(r)$$

This approach smooths out local variations and has been widely adopted in benchmark evaluations.

6.4.5 Mean Average Precision (mAP)

For multi-class or multi-category problems, we compute AP independently for each class, then average:

$$\text{mAP} = \frac{1}{C} \sum_{c=1}^{C} \text{AP}_c$$

where \( C \) is the total number of classes. mAP is the standard evaluation metric for object detection challenges (PASCAL VOC, COCO, ImageNet).

7. Bias-Variance Decomposition 7. 偏差-方差分解 7. 偏差-方差分解 7. 偏差-方差分解

The total expected error of a model can be decomposed into three irreducible components, providing deep insight into why models fail and how to improve them.

7.1 The Decomposition

$$E\left[(y - \hat{f}(x))^2\right] = \underbrace{\left(f(x) - E[\hat{f}(x)]\right)^2}_{\text{Bias}^2} + \underbrace{E\left[\left(\hat{f}(x) - E[\hat{f}(x)]\right)^2\right]}_{\text{Variance}} + \underbrace{\sigma^2}_{\text{Irreducible Noise}}$$
  • Bias²: How far is the average prediction from the true function? Measures systematic error. High bias → underfitting (model is too simple).
  • Variance: How much do predictions fluctuate across different training sets? Measures sensitivity to data. High variance → overfitting (model is too complex).
  • Noise (\( \sigma^2 \)): Irreducible error inherent in the data. No model can reduce this.

7.2 Mathematical Derivation

Assume the true data-generating process is \( y = f(x) + \epsilon \), where \( E[\epsilon] = 0 \) and \( \text{Var}(\epsilon) = \sigma^2 \). The noise \( \epsilon \) is independent of \( \hat{f} \).

经过规范化，Hard-Margin SVM 变为： $$ \max_{w,b} \frac{1}{\|w\|} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad y_i(w^T x_i+b)\ge 1,\ \forall i $$
$$E\left[(y - \hat{f})^2\right] = E\left[(f + \epsilon - \hat{f})^2\right]$$
$$= E\left[(f - \hat{f})^2\right] + E[\epsilon^2] + 2\,E\left[\epsilon(f - \hat{f})\right]$$

Since \( \epsilon \) is independent of \( \hat{f} \) and \( E[\epsilon] = 0 \), the cross term vanishes: \( E[\epsilon(f - \hat{f})] = 0 \). Also, \( E[\epsilon^2] = \sigma^2 \). So:

$$= E\left[(f - \hat{f})^2\right] + \sigma^2$$

Now add and subtract \( E[\hat{f}] \) inside the first term:

$$E\left[(f - \hat{f})^2\right] = E\left[\left((f - E[\hat{f}]) + (E[\hat{f}] - \hat{f})\right)^2\right]$$
$$= E\left[(f - E[\hat{f}])^2\right] + E\left[(E[\hat{f}] - \hat{f})^2\right] + 2\,E\left[(f - E[\hat{f}])(E[\hat{f}] - \hat{f})\right]$$

Since \( f \) is a constant (the true function) and \( E[E[\hat{f}] - \hat{f}] = 0 \), the cross term vanishes again. Thus:

$$= \underbrace{(f - E[\hat{f}])^2}_{\text{Bias}^2} + \underbrace{E\left[(\hat{f} - E[\hat{f}])^2\right]}_{\text{Variance}} + \sigma^2$$

Practical Implications

  • High Bias (Underfitting): Model is too simple to capture the true pattern. Training error is high. Remedy: increase model complexity, add features, reduce regularization.
  • High Variance (Overfitting): Model fits the training data too closely, including noise. Training error is low but test error is high. Remedy: get more data, simplify model, increase regularization, use ensembles.
  • Bias-Variance Trade-off: Increasing model complexity reduces bias but increases variance. The optimal model minimizes total error (Bias² + Variance + Noise).

8. Summary & Key Takeaways 8. 本讲总结

  1. Hard-Margin SVM: Maximize the geometric margin between classes. The functional margin introduces label information; the geometric margin removes scale ambiguity. Anchor normalization (constant 1) eliminates parameter redundancy. The result is a convex QP with a global optimum.
  2. Soft-Margin SVM: Introduces slack variables \( \xi_i \) to handle noise and overlap. The penalty coefficient \( C \) controls the trade-off between margin width and training error. Equivalent to Hinge Loss + \( L_2 \) regularization.
  3. Dual Formulation: The Lagrangian dual depends only on dot products, enabling the kernel trick. KKT conditions identify support vectors (points with \( \alpha_i > 0 \)).
  4. Kernel Trick: Replace dot products with kernel functions to implicitly operate in high-dimensional spaces. RBF kernel is the default choice. Mercer's theorem guarantees kernel validity via symmetry and PSD conditions.
  5. Model Evaluation: Use K-fold cross-validation for robust performance estimation. Stratified K-fold for imbalanced data. Nested CV for unbiased hyperparameter tuning. Never touch the test set until the end.
  6. Performance Metrics: Confusion matrix → Precision/Recall/F1. ROC-AUC for balanced data; PR curves for imbalanced data. AP summarizes PR curve quality; mAP averages AP across classes.
  7. Bias-Variance: Total error = Bias² + Variance + Noise. High bias → underfitting; high variance → overfitting. The optimal model balances the two.
"SVM's elegance lies in converting a geometric intuition (maximum margin) into a convex optimization problem, then lifting it to infinite dimensions via kernels — all while maintaining global optimality guarantees."
