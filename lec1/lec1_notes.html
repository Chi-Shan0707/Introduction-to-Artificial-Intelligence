2. Defining Artificial Intelligence 人工智能的定义

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that aims to create systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. But what exactly counts as “intelligent”? The textbook by Russell & Norvig provides a widely accepted classification of four approaches to defining AI:

Key Distinction: The human-centric approaches (left column) try to mimic or match human behavior and reasoning processes. The rationalist approaches (right column) focus on ideal behavior — what a perfectly logical agent should do, regardless of how humans actually think.

3. The Turing Test 图灵测试

Proposed by Alan Turing in his seminal 1950 paper “Computing Machinery and Intelligence,” the Imitation Game (模仿游戏) defines intelligence not by how a machine thinks, but by whether its behavior is indistinguishable from a human. A computer passes the test if a human interrogator cannot reliably tell whether they are conversing with a human or a machine through a text-only channel.

“We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done.” — Alan Turing, 1950
Components required to pass the Turing Test:
  • Natural Language Processing (自然语言处理) — to communicate fluently in natural language
  • Knowledge Representation (知识表示) — to store and retrieve information
  • Automated Reasoning (自动推理) — to answer questions and draw conclusions
  • Machine Learning (机器学习) — to adapt to new circumstances and patterns
  • Computer Vision (计算机视觉) — (added later) to perceive visual inputs if the test includes image interaction
Criticism of the Turing Test: The Chinese Room Argument (中文房间论证) by John Searle (1980) argues that passing the Turing Test does not imply true understanding — a system can manipulate symbols without any comprehension of their meaning. This remains one of the most debated philosophical objections to strong AI.

4. Evolution of Computing Paradigms 计算范式演进

The central narrative of AI is a paradigm shift in how we compute. From manually designed algorithms to machines that learn their own representations, the evolution can be summarized in three stages:

The Three Paradigm Shifts 计算范式的三次转变

Paradigm Description Era
Standard Computation
(标准计算)		 Humans design algorithms to process data 1940s–1960s
Logic Computation
(逻辑计算)		 Humans design symbolic rules for logical inference 1960s–1990s
Machine Computation
(机器计算: ML/DL)		 Machines automatically learn representations and rules from data 1990s–present

The XOR Problem — A Pivotal Moment 异或问题

In 1969, Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert published Perceptrons, in which they proved that a single-layer perceptron (单层感知机) cannot solve the non-linear XOR (exclusive-or) problem. This result had a devastating effect on neural network research and contributed directly to the first AI winter.

The XOR truth table is defined as follows:

\(x_1\) \(x_2\) \(x_1 \oplus x_2\)
000
011
101
110
$$\text{XOR}(x_1, x_2) = (x_1 \lor x_2) \land \lnot(x_1 \land x_2)$$
Why XOR matters: XOR is not linearly separable (非线性可分). No single straight line can separate the positive examples \((0,1)\) and \((1,0)\) from the negative examples \((0,0)\) and \((1,1)\). This proved that single-layer networks have fundamental limitations, and motivated the development of multi-layer networks (多层网络) and representation learning (表征学习).

The realization shifted focus towards multi-layer networks and the importance of learning representations (表征) rather than just simple mappings. In traditional machine learning, we go from measurement space (测量空间) → feature space (特征空间) → category space (类别空间). The bottleneck is always in the middle step — feature design.

5. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning 机器学习与深度学习对比

The transition from traditional Machine Learning (ML) to Deep Learning (DL) represents a fundamental change in how we handle features. In the traditional paradigm, humans must manually engineer features (人工特征工程). In deep learning, the model automatically discovers features from raw data through multiple layers of abstraction.

Aspect Traditional Machine Learning
(传统机器学习)		 Deep Learning
(深度学习)
Feature Engineering Manual / Hand-crafted (requires expert knowledge)
人工特征提取 + 分类器		 Automated representation learning
端到端自动学习特征
Domain Dependency High — each task requires domain-specific feature design
任务一变，特征通常要重做		 Low — same architecture works across domains
Data Dependency Works well with small data + good features Requires large-scale data and compute
Performance Scaling Plateaus as data increases (天花板已现) Continues to improve with more data (数据越多越好)
Interpretability High — “White box” (可解释性强) Low — “Black box” (可解释性差)
Representative Algorithms SVM, Decision Trees, KNN, Logistic Regression CNN, RNN, Transformer, GAN, Diffusion

The Traditional ML Pipeline 传统机器学习范式

In the traditional paradigm, the workflow is iterative and manual:

  1. Collect and annotate data (e.g., a few hundred iris flower images)
    2. - 2006年深度学习突破，Hinton提出深度信念网络 - 大数据时代提供海量训练数据 - GPU计算能力大幅提升 - ImageNet竞赛突破，AlphaGo战胜李世石等里程碑 - 大语言模型GPT系列引爆生成式AI热潮
  2. Observe the data and carefully design features — shape, color, texture; classic feature descriptors include SIFT, HOG, Gabor, LBP, Haar (绞尽脑汁选择或设计特征)
  3. Extract features from images, select a classifier, and train
  4. If results are unsatisfactory, return to step 2 and redesign features
Bottleneck: The ceiling of traditional ML has been reached (天花板已现). Each incremental improvement in feature design becomes progressively harder. This is the “cold weapon era” (冷兵器时代) of AI — researchers must forge stronger and more clever features by hand.

The Deep Learning Paradigm 深度学习范式

Deep Learning flips the pipeline: instead of humans designing features, the model learns features from raw data:

  1. Collect and annotate a larger dataset (thousands of images)
  2. Define network architecture, optimizer, and loss function
  3. Let the machine “rack its brains” (交给机器绞尽脑汁) to learn features and make predictions

Deep Learning utilizes multiple layers of neural networks to automatically extract hierarchical features — moving from low-level edges (低级边缘) in early layers to high-level semantic objects (高级语义) in deeper layers. This paradigm shift is what Yann LeCun, Geoffrey Hinton, and Yoshua Bengio were recognized for when they received the 2018 Turing Award for “conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing.”

6. The 6. “Three Peaks and Two Valleys” of AI 人工智能的“三起两落”

The history of AI is not a smooth upward curve — it is marked by dramatic cycles of extreme optimism followed by disappointment and funding cuts known as “AI Winters” (AI 寒冬). Understanding this non-linear history is essential for appreciating where we are today.

Phase Period Key Driver Chinese
1st Peak 1956–1970 Symbolic AI, Perceptron optimism 第一起：黄金时代
1st Valley 1970–1980 XOR proof, combinatorial explosion, funding cuts 第一落：第一次寒冬
2nd Peak 1980–1987 Expert systems, backpropagation revival 第二起：专家系统时代
2nd Valley 1987–2006 Expert system collapse, hardware limits 第二落：第二次寒冬

7. First Peak: The Golden Age (1956–1970) 第一起：黄金时代

The Dartmouth Workshop 达特茅斯会议

In the summer of 1956, John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and Nathaniel Rochester organized a workshop at Dartmouth College. This event is widely considered the official birth of AI as a field (人工智能作为独立学科的诞生). The attendees proposed that “every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it.”

Early Breakthroughs

  • Logic Theorist (1956): Allen Newell and Herbert Simon’s program that could prove mathematical theorems — the first AI program.
  • Perceptron (1957): Frank Rosenblatt’s single-layer neural network generated enormous excitement about the potential of neural computation.
  • Checkers programs: Early game-playing AI demonstrated that machines could learn from experience.
  • General Problem Solver (1959): Attempted to solve general problems using means-ends analysis.
The Optimism: Scholars predicted that machines would match human intelligence within 20 years. Government agencies poured funding into AI research, and the field entered a period of extraordinary confidence.

8. First Valley: The First AI Winter (1970–1980) 第一落：第一次寒冬

Causes of the Collapse

  • The XOR Proof (1969): Minsky and Papert’s Perceptrons demonstrated that single-layer neural networks cannot solve non-linearly separable problems, dampening enthusiasm for connectionism.
  • Combinatorial Explosion (组合爆炸): Early AI systems could handle small toy problems but failed catastrophically when scaled to real-world complexity. The number of possible states grew exponentially.
  • Overpromising and Underdelivering: The grand promises of the 1960s were not met, leading to disillusionment.
  • The Lighthill Report (1973): Sir James Lighthill’s devastating report to the UK Parliament concluded that AI had “failed to achieve its grand objectives,” leading to massive funding cuts in the UK.
  • DARPA Funding Cuts: The US Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency also sharply reduced AI funding.
Lesson: The first AI winter teaches us that theoretical elegance alone is insufficient — AI systems must demonstrate practical scalability. This lesson would repeat itself in the second winter.

9. Second Peak: Expert Systems & Connectionism (1980–1987) 第二起：专家系统时代

Expert Systems 专家系统

Expert systems encode human domain expertise into IF-THEN rules (if-then 规则). They represented the knowledge engineering approach (知识工程) to AI.

System Domain Significance
DENDRAL (1965) Chemical molecular structure analysis First expert system
MYCIN (1976) Blood infection diagnosis Demonstrated medical AI potential
PROSPECTOR (1977) Geological mineral exploration Developed at Stanford
XCON / R1 (1980) Computer configuration Saved DEC millions of dollars

The knowledge period (知识期, mid-1970s onwards) held that to make machines intelligent, one must endow them with knowledge. E.A. Feigenbaum, the “father of knowledge engineering,” received the Turing Award in 1994 for this work.

The Neural Renaissance 神经网络复兴

Simultaneously, connectionism experienced a revival:

- 1956年达特茅斯会议标志着人工智能作为独立学科的诞生 - 感知机的提出让人们对神经网络充满希望 - 逻辑推理、定理证明等早期AI研究取得进展 - 乐观主义盛行，学者们认为20年内机器将超越人类
  • Hopfield Networks (1982): John Hopfield showed that neural networks could serve as associative memories, reigniting interest in neural computation.
  • Backpropagation (1986): Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams demonstrated that the backpropagation algorithm (反向传播算法, BP) could efficiently train multi-layer networks, solving the two-layer network parameter learning problem.
  • Japan’s Fifth Generation Computer Project (1982): An ambitious national project to build AI hardware, driving global investment.
Backpropagation in a nutshell: The BP algorithm computes the gradient of the loss function with respect to each weight by applying the chain rule (链式法则) from the output layer back to the input layer. The weight update rule is:
$$w_{ij} \leftarrow w_{ij} - \eta \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial w_{ij}}$$
where \(\eta\) is the learning rate and \(\mathcal{L}\) is the loss function.

However, “gradient vanishing” (梯度消失) problems meant that neural network methods only achieved limited success at this stage.

10. Second Valley: The Second AI Winter (1987–2006) 第二落：第二次寒冬

Why Expert Systems Failed 专家系统陷入低谷的原因

Expert systems achieved some success in narrow domains, but ultimately collapsed for several fundamental reasons:

  • Narrow domain knowledge: Domain knowledge was very narrow and difficult to transfer to other fields (领域知识很狭窄，难以应用于其他领域).
  • Scalability crisis: As systems grew larger, managing them and providing data effectively became increasingly challenging (系统越来越大，管理越来越难).
  • No learning capability: Expert systems could not learn over time — they required constant manual updates to the underlying logic model (不会随时间学习，必须不断手动更新).
  • Maintenance costs: They were extremely expensive to maintain and update.
Core Problem: The human world is highly complex. Even in vertical domains, many situations cannot be completely described by existing rules (人类世界高度复杂，即使是垂直领域，很多也难以用现有规则完备描述).

Meanwhile: The Rise of Statistical ML

During this “valley,” statistical machine learning methods quietly rose to prominence. The 1990s saw the dominance of Support Vector Machines (SVM) (支持向量机) and other kernel methods. The focus shifted from hand-crafted rules to data-driven statistical approaches.

Computational Intelligence: “Brute Force” Approaches

Computing power increased rapidly, enabling “brute force” (大力出奇迹) approaches:

  • 1997 — Deep Blue: IBM’s chess computer defeated world champion Garry Kasparov. Deep Blue weighed 1.4 tons, had 32 nodes with 256 specialized processors, and could evaluate over 200 million positions per second. It won through exhaustive search, not understanding.
  • Search Engines: Google’s PageRank algorithm demonstrated that massive data + clever indexing could produce intelligent-seeming behavior. Commercial search engines index hundreds of billions of pages.
PageRank Algorithm (PageRank 算法): Based on two key hypotheses:
  1. Quantity Hypothesis (数量假设): A page with more incoming links is more important.
  2. Quality Hypothesis (质量假设): Links from high-quality pages carry more weight.
PageRank starts by assigning equal importance scores, then iteratively updates scores via recursive computation until convergence.

Convolutional Neural Networks: A Quiet Beginning

In 1998, Yann LeCun et al. proposed LeNet-5, which established the basic structure of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) (卷积神经网络). It achieved good results on handwritten digit recognition (MNIST), but at the time there were competing methods with comparable performance and more elegant mathematical properties, so this work did not attract wide attention.

11. Third Peak: The Deep Learning Era (2006–Present) 第三起：深度学习时代

The Catalyst: Three Converging Factors

The deep learning revolution was not caused by a single breakthrough, but by the convergence of three factors:

Factor Description Chinese
Algorithmic Breakthroughs Hinton’s Deep Belief Networks (2006) showed how to train deep architectures; ReLU, dropout, batch normalization followed 算法突破
Big Data Internet-scale datasets (ImageNet: 14M+ labeled images) became available 大数据积累
GPU Computing Graphics processors provided massive parallel compute at low cost 算力提升 (GPU)

Li Fei-Fei and ImageNet 李飞飞和ImageNet

In 2006, Li Fei-Fei (李飞飞) observed that the entire AI community was focused on better algorithms but ignoring data quality. Her insight: “If the data used cannot reflect real-world conditions, even the best algorithms are useless.” Her solution was to construct a large-scale, high-quality dataset — ImageNet, containing over 14 million labeled images across 20,000+ categories. This dataset became the catalyst that proved deep learning’s superiority.

Key Insight: Li Fei-Fei’s contribution was not an algorithm — it was a dataset. This underscores a crucial lesson: in the deep learning era, data is as important as algorithms (数据和算法同等重要).

12. Landmark Events in Modern AI 12. 现代AI里程碑事件 现代AI里程碑事件

AlexNet (2012) — The Deep Learning Watershed

Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton’s AlexNet won the 2012 ImageNet competition by a huge margin, reducing the top-5 error rate from ~26% to ~16%. This was the moment the AI community took deep learning seriously. AlexNet used GPUs for training, ReLU activation, and dropout regularization — techniques that became standard.

AlphaGo (2016) — Defeating the World Champion

DeepMind’s AlphaGo defeated Lee Sedol (李世石) 4–1 in Go, a game long considered beyond the reach of brute-force search due to its astronomical branching factor (\(\sim 250\) moves per position vs. \(\sim 35\) in chess). The evolution continued:

System Key Innovation Result
AlphaGo (2016) Historical game data + domain knowledge + rules 4:1 vs. Lee Sedol, 3:0 vs. Ke Jie (柯洁)
AlphaGo Zero (2017) Self-play only, no human data (无师自通) 100:0 vs. AlphaGo
AlphaZero (2017) Same algorithm for Go, Chess, Shogi Dominates all three games from scratch
MuZero (2019) Learns rules from scratch, fewer search steps Stronger than AlphaZero with less computation

The CNN Pioneer Turing Award (2018)

In 2018, the Turing Award was jointly awarded to Yann LeCun, Geoffrey Hinton, and Yoshua Bengio for “conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing.”

AlphaFold (2020) — Solving Protein Folding

DeepMind’s AlphaFold solved the 50-year-old protein structure prediction problem, predicting 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences with near-experimental accuracy. This earned a share of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.

Transformer — The Architecture Revolution (2017)

The paper “Attention Is All You Need” (Vaswani et al., 2017) introduced the Transformer architecture, which replaced recurrent networks with the self-attention mechanism (自注意力机制). This became the foundation for BERT, GPT, and all modern large language models.

How Self-Attention Works (自注意力机制):
  1. Represent the input sequence as a vector sequence
  2. Compute relevance (attention scores) between each token and every other token
  3. Use the learned relevance to produce context-aware representations
The attention computation uses Query (Q), Key (K), and Value (V) matrices:
$$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$

13. Hinton’s View: Digital vs. Biological Intelligence 数字计算与生物计算

Geoffrey Hinton, the “Godfather of AI” (AI 教父), provides a provocative comparison between digital and biological intelligence, particularly in his 2024 Oxford lecture:

“Digital computation requires a lot of energy but makes it very easy for agents that have the same model of the world to share what they have learned by sharing weights or gradients. Biological computation requires much less energy but is much worse at sharing knowledge between agents.”
Dimension Digital Intelligence (数字智能) Biological Intelligence (生物智能)
Energy Consumption High (large GPU clusters) Low (human brain: ~20W)
Knowledge Sharing Instant: copy-paste weights across agents Slow: natural language communication
Knowledge Scale GPT-4 knows thousands of times more than any single human, using only ~2% as many parameters Each individual has limited knowledge
Learning Efficiency Requires massive data and compute Can learn from very few examples
Parallelism Data parallelism across thousands of GPUs Massive parallelism within one brain

Hinton Hinton的观点’s Conclusion

“If energy is cheap, digital computation is just better.” — Digital intelligence’s superpower is the ability to replicate, share, and aggregate knowledge (知识可复制、可传播、可叠加) across agents at scale. This insight has profound implications for the engineering direction of large models in the era of generative AI.

The Scalability of Digital Intelligence:
  • Knowledge Replication: GPT-4 contains thousands of times more knowledge than any human because it can aggregate data from millions of sources simultaneously.
  • Efficiency of Sharing: Digital agents can “copy-paste” weights, while humans must use slow natural language communication (人类必须通过缓慢的自然语言交流).
  • Implication: If energy becomes cheap (through nuclear fusion, solar, etc.), digital computation is fundamentally superior in knowledge accumulation.

ChatGPT — A Watershed Moment 里程碑时刻

Released on November 30, 2022, ChatGPT (Chat Generative Pre-trained Transformer) reached 1 million users within 5 days and an estimated 100 million monthly active users within 2 months — making it the fastest-growing consumer application in history.

ChatGPT’s Core Technology — RLHF (人类反馈强化学习):
  1. Pre-train a large language model on massive text data
    2. - 2006年深度学习突破，Hinton提出深度信念网络 - 大数据时代提供海量训练数据 - GPU计算能力大幅提升 - ImageNet竞赛突破，AlphaGo战胜李世石等里程碑 - 大语言模型GPT系列引爆生成式AI热潮
  2. Collect human feedback: humans rank model outputs by quality
  3. Train a Reward Model from human preferences
  4. Optimize via Reinforcement Learning (PPO) to align the model with human preferences
The process: 学说人话 → 学人偏好 → 反馈强化

Diffusion Models 扩散模型

Inspired by statistical physics, diffusion models work by:

  1. Forward process: Systematically corrupt data by adding noise step by step
  2. Reverse process: Learn to denoise — recovering structure from pure noise
$$\text{Forward: } q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}(x_t; \sqrt{1-\beta_t}\, x_{t-1},\, \beta_t I)$$

Key milestones: DALL·E 2 (2022), Stable Diffusion (2022), DALL·E 3 (2023), Sora (2024). These models have shown extraordinary cross-modal generation capabilities, impacting art creation, film special effects, game design, and more.

Sora — Video Generation 视频生成

OpenAI’s Sora (2024) generates up to 60-second photorealistic videos from text prompts, representing another revolutionary step after ChatGPT. It demonstrates the ability to simulate complex physical scenes with coherent motion, lighting, and reflections.

The next frontier is embodied AI (具身智能) — intelligent agents that can perceive and act in the physical world through robotic bodies, bridging the gap between digital intelligence and physical reality.

17. Summary 本讲总结

Core Takeaways 核心要点

  1. Paradigm Shift: AI has evolved from manually programmed logic (逻辑计算) to data-driven representation learning (表征学习). The shift from “humans design features” to “machines learn features” is the defining transition.
  2. Non-linear History: The history of AI is marked by “Three Peaks and Two Valleys” (三起两落), driven by breakthroughs in algorithms, hardware, and data availability — and by the inevitable disappointments that follow overpromising.
  3. Deep Learning’s Advantage: The core advantage of deep learning is its ability to learn multi-level features automatically, bypassing the “feature engineering bottleneck” (特征工程瓶颈). This was enabled by the convergence of algorithms (backpropagation, ReLU, attention), data (ImageNet), and compute (GPUs).
  4. Digital Intelligence’s Superpower: As Hinton argues, digital computation’s fundamental advantage is the ability to share and aggregate knowledge across agents at scale (知识可复制、可传播、可叠加). If energy becomes cheap, digital intelligence is unambiguously superior in knowledge accumulation.
  5. Generative AI Revolution: The Transformer architecture (2017), pre-training + RLHF (ChatGPT, 2022), and diffusion models have created a new paradigm where AI can understand, generate, and reason across modalities.
  6. The Future is Multimodal and Embodied: Modern AI is moving from text-only to multimodal understanding and generation, and from purely digital systems to embodied agents that interact with the physical world.
