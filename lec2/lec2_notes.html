1. Formal Logic Foundations (形式逻辑)

Formal logic (形式逻辑) is the discipline that studies the general laws of thought. Rather than focusing on the content of knowledge, it examines whether the structure of reasoning is sound. This is precisely the rigorous framework that machines need in order to "reason."

Core Idea (核心理念): Logic does not merely study what we know; more importantly, it studies whether the structure of thought, the process of inference, and the conclusions drawn are rigorous and valid.
The Three Pillars of Formal Logic (三个关键词):
  1. Laws (规律): All things develop according to inherent logic; behind the complex phenomena of the world, there are often rules to follow.
  2. Inference (推理): Drawing conclusions from premises. The most famous form is the Syllogism (三段论).
  3. Judgment (判断): The ability to analyze, judge, and distinguish the truth of given information.

The Classic Syllogism (经典三段论)

A syllogism is a fundamental form of deductive reasoning consisting of a major premise, a minor premise, and a conclusion:

**符号化例子**： - "lucy和lily是姐妹" → $\text{Sister}(\text{lucy}, \text{lily})$ - "北京是中国的首都" → $\text{CapitalOf}(\text{Beijing}, \text{China})$
1. All men are mortal.   (Major Premise / 大前提)
2. Socrates is a man.   (Minor Premise / 小前提)
3. Therefore, Socrates is mortal.   (Conclusion / 结论)

While this looks trivial, the challenge for AI is: how do we formalize this so a machine can perform the same reasoning? This leads us to propositional logic and, ultimately, predicate logic.

2. Propositional Logic (命题逻辑)

2.1 What Is a Proposition? (命题的定义)

A proposition (命题) is a declarative sentence that can be judged to be either true (真, 1) or false (假, 0). Propositional logic treats full sentences as atomic units — it does not look inside them.

ExampleTypeVerdict
"Beijing is the capital of China."Declarative✓ True proposition
"2 + 2 = 5"Declarative✓ False proposition
"Are you in a meeting?"Interrogative✗ Not a proposition
"Please leave."Imperative✗ Not a proposition
"I am lying right now."Paradox✗ Not a proposition (self-contradiction)

2.2 Atomic vs. Compound Propositions

Atomic propositions (原子命题) are indivisible units, denoted \(P, Q, R, \ldots\). Compound propositions (复合命题) are formed by combining atomic propositions with logical connectives (逻辑连接词).

ConnectiveSymbolFormTruth Condition
AND (Conjunction / 合取) \(\land\) \(P \land Q\) True only if both are true
OR (Disjunction / 析取) \(\lor\) \(P \lor Q\) True if at least one is true
NOT (Negation / 否定) \(\lnot\) \(\lnot P\) Inverts the truth value
Implication (蕴含) \(\rightarrow\) \(P \rightarrow Q\) False only when \(P\) is true and \(Q\) is false
Biconditional (双向蕴含) \(\leftrightarrow\) \(P \leftrightarrow Q\) True if both have the same truth value
XOR (Exclusive OR / 异或) \(\oplus\) \(P \oplus Q\) True if exactly one is true

2.3 Complete Truth Table for \(P \rightarrow Q\)

The implication connective is often the most confusing. Think of it as a promise: "If it rains (\(P\)), I will pick you up (\(Q\))." Only one scenario breaks the promise — raining but not picking up.

\(P\)\(Q\)\(P \rightarrow Q\)Interpretation
TTTRained, picked up — promise kept
TFFRained, did NOT pick up — promise broken
FTTDid not rain, picked up anyway — no violation
FFTDid not rain, did not pick up — no violation
Why is "F → T" true? The promise was "if it rains, I'll pick you up." It didn't rain, so the promise was never tested. Picking someone up voluntarily does not violate the promise. In logic, an implication with a false antecedent is vacuously true.

2.6 Limitations of Propositional Logic 命题逻辑

Key Limitation (局限性): Propositional logic cannot look inside a sentence. It treats "Socrates is a man" as a single symbol \(P\), and "All men are mortal" as another symbol \(Q\). It cannot express the relationship between "Socrates" (individual) and "Man" (category).

Consider the Syllogism:
\(\alpha:\) All men are mortal    \(\beta:\) Socrates is a man    \(\gamma:\) Socrates is mortal
We can write \(\alpha \land \beta \Rightarrow \gamma\), but this fails to explain why the conclusion follows. The real reason is the shared concept of "Human" — something propositional logic cannot capture.

3. Predicate Logic — First-Order Logic (谓词逻辑 / FOL)

Predicate logic (谓词逻辑), also called First-Order Logic (一阶逻辑, FOL), extends propositional logic by breaking sentences into individuals (个体), predicates (谓词), and quantifiers (量词). This enables the expression of properties of objects and relationships between them.

3.1 Core Components

3.1.1 Individuals (个体)

Objects that exist independently. They can be concrete or abstract:

**3. 量词（Quantifier）** - **全称量词** $\forall$（对所有、任意）：$\forall x. P(x)$ = 对所有的x，都有性质P - **存在量词** $\exists$（存在某个）：$\exists x. P(x)$ = 存在某个x，具有性质P
  • Concrete: lucy, Socrates, Beijing
  • Abstract: 3, the number π

3.1.2 Predicates (谓词)

A predicate describes an attribute of an individual or a relationship between individuals.

| 连接词 | 符号 | 形式 | 真值条件 | |--------|------|------|---------| | 与（合取）| $\land$ | $P \land Q$ | 两个都真才真 | | 或（析取）| $\lor$ | $P \lor Q$ | 至少一个真就真 | | 非（否定）| $\lnot$ | $\lnot P$ | 原命题假则结果真 | | 蕴含 | $\rightarrow$ | $P \rightarrow Q$ | 前真后假时为假，其余为真 | | 双向蕴含 | $\leftrightarrow$ | $P \leftrightarrow Q$ | 同真或同假时为真 |
TypeFormExampleMeaning
Unary (一元谓词) \(P(x)\) Human(Socrates) Socrates is human
King(Richard) Richard is a king
Binary (二元谓词) \(R(x,y)\) Sister(lucy, lily) lucy and lily are sisters
CapitalOf(Beijing, China) Beijing is the capital of China
Ternary (多元谓词) \(R(x,y,z)\) Between(x, y, z) x is between y and z

3.1.3 Quantifiers (量词)

Quantifiers bind variables to express statements over all or some elements of a domain.

**3. 量词（Quantifier）** - **全称量词** $\forall$（对所有、任意）：$\forall x. P(x)$ = 对所有的x，都有性质P - **存在量词** $\exists$（存在某个）：$\exists x. P(x)$ = 存在某个x，具有性质P
QuantifierSymbolReadingExample
Universal (全称量词) \(\forall\) "For all \(x\)…" \(\forall x.\; \text{Human}(x) \rightarrow \text{Mortal}(x)\)
Existential (存在量词) \(\exists\) "There exists an \(x\)…" \(\exists x.\; \text{Prime}(x) \land \text{Even}(x)\)

3.2 The Ambiguity of Natural Language (自然语言的歧义性)

Case Study: "All people love some person"

This single English sentence has two completely different formalizations depending on quantifier order:

Interpretation 1: \(\forall x\; \exists y.\; \text{Love}(x, y)\)
"For every person, there exists someone they love." — Each person may love a different person.

**3. 量词（Quantifier）** - **全称量词** $\forall$（对所有、任意）：$\forall x. P(x)$ = 对所有的x，都有性质P - **存在量词** $\exists$（存在某个）：$\exists x. P(x)$ = 存在某个x，具有性质P

Interpretation 2: \(\exists y\; \forall x.\; \text{Love}(x, y)\)
"There exists a person whom everyone loves." — One universally beloved individual.

**符号化例子**： - "lucy和lily是姐妹" → $\text{Sister}(\text{lucy}, \text{lily})$ - "北京是中国的首都" → $\text{CapitalOf}(\text{Beijing}, \text{China})$

The order of quantifiers matters! This is a major source of ambiguity in natural language that FOL resolves precisely.

**符号化例子**： - "lucy和lily是姐妹" → $\text{Sister}(\text{lucy}, \text{lily})$ - "北京是中国的首都" → $\text{CapitalOf}(\text{Beijing}, \text{China})$

3.3 The Syllogism in FOL

Now we can formalize the classic syllogism and explain why the conclusion follows:

$$\forall x.\; \text{Human}(x) \rightarrow \text{Mortal}(x)$$ $$\text{Human}(\text{Socrates})$$ $$\therefore\; \text{Mortal}(\text{Socrates})$$

The reasoning is now transparent: Socrates satisfies the predicate \(\text{Human}()\), and everything satisfying \(\text{Human}()\) also satisfies \(\text{Mortal}()\). This is a direct application of Universal Instantiation (全称实例化) followed by Modus Ponens (假言推理).

Modus Ponens (假言推理): If \(P \rightarrow Q\) is true and \(P\) is true, then \(Q\) must be true. This is the single most important inference rule in logic.
\(\frac{P \rightarrow Q, \quad P}{\therefore\; Q}\)

4. The Four Laws of Logic (逻辑四大规律)

Formal logic demands that the process of thought obey certain inviolable laws. These are the foundational constraints on rational reasoning:

LawChineseContentExample
Law of Identity (同一律) 同一律 In the same process of thought, a concept must remain consistent throughout. You cannot say "Apple is a fruit" in one sentence and "Apple is a phone brand" in the next within the same argument.
Law of Non-Contradiction (矛盾律) 矛盾律 A proposition and its negation cannot both be true at the same time. You cannot assert "I ate" and "I did not eat" simultaneously.
Law of Excluded Middle (排中律) 排中律 Of two contradictory propositions, one must be true. There is no middle ground. A crime is either intentional or not intentional; it cannot be both or neither.
Law of Sufficient Reason (充足理由律) 充足理由律 Any judgment must be supported by sufficient reasons or evidence. "Smart kids are always good at playing" — this conclusion lacks sufficient logical grounds.
Why do these matter for AI? These laws ensure consistency in knowledge bases. A knowledge base that violates the Law of Non-Contradiction (containing both \(P\) and \(\lnot P\)) can derive any statement, a problem known as ex falso quodlibet (principle of explosion). Automated reasoners must detect and resolve contradictions.

5. Curry–Howard Correspondence (Curry-Howard 对应)

"Is this related to lambda calculus and the underlying theory of computation?" — Yes, deeply!

There is a profound and beautiful connection between formal logic and computation, known as the Curry–Howard Correspondence (Curry-Howard 对应). It reveals that logic and programming are, at their core, the same thing.

The Curry–Howard Dictionary

Logic (逻辑) Programming (编程)
Proposition (命题)Type (类型)
Proof (证明)Program (程序)
\(A \rightarrow B\) (implication)\(A \to B\) (function type)
A proof of \(A\)A value of type A
Proving \(A \rightarrow B\)Implementing function A -> B
Using a proof (使用证明)Function application (函数应用)

Intuition

  • Logic = symbolic manipulation + inference rules (how to derive new knowledge from old)
  • Lambda Calculus = function abstraction + application mechanism (how to represent computation)
    • **关键区别**： - 谓词逻辑：关注**可量化的对象与关系**（what exists） - Lambda演算：关注**函数与计算步骤**（how to compute） - 要把FOL完全编码成$\lambda$演算需要**高阶逻辑**或**类型论**
  • Both are doing formalization, symbolization, and rule-based transformation.

Key Distinction 关键区别

FrameworkFocusQuestion
Predicate Logic (FOL)Quantifiable objects & relationsWhat exists?
Lambda CalculusFunctions & computation stepsHow to compute?

Encoding full FOL into \(\lambda\)-calculus requires higher-order logic (高阶逻辑) or type theory (类型论).

6.2 The "Promise" Interpretation of Implication

"If it rains today (\(P\)), I will pick you up (\(Q\))." — This corresponds to \(P \rightarrow Q\).

7. Information Entropy & Information Gain (信息熵与信息增益)

Information theory provides a quantitative tool for measuring uncertainty — entropy (熵). This is the bridge where logic meets machine learning.

7.1 Shannon Entropy (香农熵)

$$H(X) = - \sum_{x} p(x)\, \log_2\, p(x)$$
  • When the distribution is uniform (maximum uncertainty), entropy is maximized.
  • When the distribution is concentrated (near-certain), entropy is minimized (approaches 0).
  • Using \(\log_2\), the unit is bits (比特).
    • > **符号小贴士：`:-` 是什么？** > 在接下来的推理推导过程中，你会看到类似 `Father(x,y) :- Couple(x,z)` 的写法。 > 这里的 `:-` 是逻辑编程语言（如经典的 Prolog）中表示“**如果**”（if）或“**被……蕴含**”（等同于数学符号 $\leftarrow$）的专用符号。 > `结论 :- 条件` 也就是前面讲的霍恩子句结构，等价于“如果满足右边的条件，左边的结论就成立”。
Intuition: Entropy measures "how surprised you are on average." A fair coin flip (\(p = 0.5\)) gives \(H = 1\) bit of surprise. A loaded coin (\(p = 0.99\)) gives \(H \approx 0.08\) bits — almost no surprise.

7.2 Information Gain (信息增益)

In supervised learning, information gain measures how much a feature reduces uncertainty about the label:

- **节点（Entity / 实体）**：图中的圆圈。代表具体的对象。比如图中的 `James`, `David`, `Ann`, `Mike`。 - **边（Relation / 关系）**：连接圆圈的黑线/红线。代表实体之间的关系。比如 `Father`, `Mother`, `Couple`（夫妻）, `Sibling`（兄弟姐妹）。
$$IG(Y, A) = H(Y) - H(Y \mid A)$$
  • \(H(Y)\): entropy of the label before knowing feature \(A\).
  • \(H(Y \mid A)\): conditional entropy — entropy of the label after observing \(A\).
  • The feature that maximizes \(IG\) is the one that "splits the data most purely."

7.3 Connection to Logic and FOIL

Key Insight:
  • Decision Trees: Choose the feature with the highest \(IG\) as the splitting criterion — the fastest entropy reduction.
  • FOIL Algorithm: Uses the same information-gain idea, but the "features" are now logical literals (谓词条件) rather than table columns. The goal is the same: make the covered examples as pure as possible.

This is the first junction where logical reasoning and information-theoretic learning converge.

8. Digital Logic & Hardware Realization (数字逻辑)

Digital logic (数字逻辑) answers the question: How do we implement Boolean algebra with electronic circuits, so that computers can truly "execute" logic?

8.1 Logic Gates (逻辑门)

Using high voltage / low voltage to represent \(1 / 0\), logical operations become physical switching circuits. The fundamental gates are:

GateSymbolFunction
AND Gate (与门)\(\land\)Output 1 only if both inputs are 1
OR Gate (或门)\(\lor\)Output 1 if at least one input is 1
NOT Gate (非门)\(\lnot\)Invert the input
NAND Gate (与非门)\(\lnot(A \land B)\)Inverted AND — universal (can build everything)
NOR Gate (或非门)\(\lnot(A \lor B)\)Inverted OR — also universal
XOR Gate (异或门)\(\oplus\)Output 1 if inputs differ
Universality of NAND: Every logic function — AND, OR, NOT, XOR — can be built from NAND gates alone. Real CPUs are built primarily from NAND/NOR gates for manufacturing efficiency.

8.2 Binary Representation

Computers encode all data in binary (二进制):

\(1001_2 = 1 \cdot 2^3 + 0 \cdot 2^2 + 0 \cdot 2^1 + 1 \cdot 2^0 = 9_{10}\)

Addition, multiplication — all ultimately decompose into bitwise Boolean operations with carry propagation.

8.4 Full Adder (全加器)

A full adder extends the half adder by including a carry-in bit \(C_{in}\) from a lower stage:

- **节点（Entity / 实体）**：图中的圆圈。代表具体的对象。比如图中的 `James`, `David`, `Ann`, `Mike`。 - **边（Relation / 关系）**：连接圆圈的黑线/红线。代表实体之间的关系。比如 `Father`, `Mother`, `Couple`（夫妻）, `Sibling`（兄弟姐妹）。
$$\text{Sum} = A \oplus B \oplus C_{in}$$ $$C_{out} = (A \land B) \lor (C_{in} \land (A \oplus B))$$

A multi-bit adder (多位加法器) chains full adders in series: each stage's \(C_{out}\) feeds into the next stage's \(C_{in}\), forming a ripple-carry adder (行波进位加法器).

The Engineering Insight

Simple local units (logic gates → adders → ALU → CPU) combine in layers of increasing complexity to build a machine that can execute any algorithm. Deep neural networks follow the same philosophy: simple modules (matrix multiplications + activation functions) composed into massive architectures — all ultimately reducible to Boolean circuits at the bottom.

10. FOIL Algorithm (FOIL 算法)

FOIL (First-Order Inductive Learner) is a classic algorithm for learning logical rules from facts. It automatically discovers human-readable inference rules from a knowledge graph using a sequential covering strategy guided by information gain.

10.1 What FOIL Produces

FOIL learns rules in the form of Horn clauses (霍恩子句):

$$\text{Father}(x, y) \leftarrow \text{Couple}(x, z) \land \text{Mother}(z, y)$$

Read: "If \(x\) and \(z\) are a couple, and \(z\) is the mother of \(y\), then \(x\) is the father of \(y\)."

最终它要学出的规则长这样（霍恩子句）： $$Father(x,y) \leftarrow Couple(x,z) \land Mother(z,y)$$ *(读作：如果 x 和 z 是夫妻，且 z 是 y 的妈妈，则推导出 x 是 y 的爸爸)*
Symbol Guide: What does :- mean?
In logic programming languages like Prolog, :- means "if" (i.e., \(\leftarrow\)). The notation
Father(x,y) :- Couple(x,z), Mother(z,y)
is equivalent to the Horn clause above: "the conclusion holds if all conditions on the right are satisfied."

10.2 Problem Setup: The Detective's Board

ComponentDescriptionExamples
Target (目标) The relation to learn rules for Father(x, y)
Positive Examples (正例) Known true instances Father(David, Mike), Father(David, Ann)
Negative Examples (反例) Known false instances Father(James, Ann) (James is female), Father(Ann, Mike)
Background Knowledge (背景知识) Other known relations in the graph Couple(x,z), Mother(z,y), Sibling(x,y)

10.3 Step-by-Step Derivation (侦探推理过程)

Step 0: Initial "Empty" Rule

Father(x, y) :- true.
// "Any pair of entities could be Father(x, y)."
// Covers ALL positive examples, but also ALL negative examples.
// Precision is terrible. We must add constraints!

Step 1: First Round — Find the Best Predicate

FOIL tries adding each candidate predicate from the background knowledge and measures information gain:

CandidateEffectInformation Gain
Sibling(x, y) Cannot distinguish fathers from non-fathers well Low
Couple(x, z) Eliminates entities with no spouse (e.g., Ann, Mike). Negative examples drop sharply. Purity increases significantly. Highest

The rule becomes:

**应用**：专家系统的规则库常由这种命题组合而成 > 如：IF 机房停电 THEN 实验无法进行
Father(x, y) :- Couple(x, z).
// "x must have a spouse z to be a candidate father."
// Remaining positives: {Father(David, Mike), Father(David, Ann), ...}
// Remaining negatives: e.g., Father(James, Ann) — James IS in Couple, so still false positive!

Step 2: Second Round — Refine Further

The rule still covers some false positives (e.g., Father(James, Ann) since James is in a Couple relation). FOIL tries adding another predicate:

CandidateEffectResult
Sibling(x, y) Does not help disambiguate Low gain
Mother(z, y) Now the rule says: x's spouse z must be y's mother.
Testing: David + James → Couple OK, Mother(James, Ann) OK ✓
James as father? Couple(James, David) OK, but Mother(David, Ann)? NO ✗
All false positives eliminated!		 Highest
Father(x, y) :- Couple(x, z), Mother(z, y).
// "If x and z are couple, and z is y's mother, then x is y's father."
// Positive examples covered: ALL ✓
// Negative examples covered: ZERO ✓
// PURITY = 100%. STOP.

Step 3: Final Learned Rule

$$\boxed{\text{Father}(x, y) \leftarrow \text{Couple}(x, z) \land \text{Mother}(z, y)}$$

Using this machine-discovered rule, we can now predict the missing red edge: Father(David, Ann).

Analogy: Decision trees find the "most informative column split" in a table. FOIL finds the "most informative relation path" in a knowledge graph. Both use information gain — they just operate on different data structures.

11. Path Ranking Algorithm (PRA, 路径排序算法)

Beyond learning explicit logical rules, there is a more "statistical ML" approach to knowledge graph reasoning: the Path Ranking Algorithm (路径排序算法, PRA).

除了显式学逻辑规则外，还有一种更“统计学习式”的知识图谱关系推理方法：**路径排序算法（Path Ranking / Path Ranking Algorithm, PRA）**。

11.1 Core Idea

Treat the paths between two entities in the graph as features, and train a classifier to predict whether a target relation holds.

- **节点（Entity / 实体）**：图中的圆圈。代表具体的对象。比如图中的 `James`, `David`, `Ann`, `Mike`。 - **边（Relation / 关系）**：连接圆圈的黑线/红线。代表实体之间的关系。比如 `Father`, `Mother`, `Couple`（夫妻）, `Sibling`（兄弟姐妹）。

11.2 Pipeline

StageDescriptionDetails
1. Feature Extraction (特征抽取) Enumerate paths from entity \(s\) to entity \(t\) Using random walk, BFS, DFS. Each unique path pattern \(P_i\) is a feature.
e.g., \(s \xrightarrow{\text{Couple}} z \xrightarrow{\text{Mother}} t\)
2. Feature Computation (特征计算) For each \((s,t)\) pair, compute feature values Binary (path exists: 0/1), count, or transition probability. Produce feature vector \(\mathbf{x}_{s,t}\).
3. Model Training (模型训练) Train a classifier on feature vectors + labels Logistic regression, linear model, etc.
4. Inference (推理) For a new \((s,t)\) pair, extract features and predict Output: probability that the target relation exists.

11.3 PRA vs. FOIL

CommonalityBoth exploit path structure in the graph for relational reasoning.

12. Probabilistic Graphical Models (概率图模型)

In the real world, many relationships are uncertain: sensors are noisy, observations are incomplete, and causal relations are probabilistic rather than absolute. Probabilistic Graphical Models (PGMs, 概率图模型) combine graph theory with probability theory to handle this uncertainty.

12.1 Two Families of PGMs

PropertyBayesian Network (贝叶斯网络)Markov Network (马尔可夫网络)
Graph typeDirected Acyclic Graph (DAG, 有向无环图)Undirected Graph (无向图)
Edge meaningCausal / generative dependencySymmetric correlation / constraint
ParameterizationConditional Probability Tables (CPT, 条件概率表)Potential functions (势函数)
Best forCausal reasoning (cause → effect)Pixel-level smoothness, spatial constraints

12.2 Bayesian Networks: Structure and Semantics

A classic example: the Grass Wetness network.

- **贝叶斯网络（Bayesian Network）**： - 有向无环图（DAG）； - 节点 = 随机变量（如“是否下雨”、“是否洒水”、“草坪是否湿”）； - 边 = 条件依赖关系； - 每个节点配一个**条件概率表(CPT)**，如 $P(\text{WetGrass} \mid \text{Rain}, \text{Sprinkler})$。
$$\text{Cloudy} \rightarrow \text{Sprinkler} \rightarrow \text{WetGrass}$$ $$\text{Cloudy} \rightarrow \text{Rain} \rightarrow \text{WetGrass}$$

Each node has a Conditional Probability Table (CPT). For instance:

**3. 量词（Quantifier）** - **全称量词** $\forall$（对所有、任意）：$\forall x. P(x)$ = 对所有的x，都有性质P - **存在量词** $\exists$（存在某个）：$\exists x. P(x)$ = 存在某个x，具有性质P
$$P(\text{WetGrass} = \text{true} \mid \text{Sprinkler}, \text{Rain})$$

12.3 Local Markov Property (局部马尔可夫性)

Key Property: Given a node's parents, it is conditionally independent of all its non-descendants.

Intuition: Once you know the direct causes, distant ancestors or unrelated nodes provide no additional information about the current variable.

This property allows the joint probability to factorize compactly:

$$P(X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} P(X_i \mid \text{Parents}(X_i))$$

12.4 Bayesian Inference (贝叶斯推理)

The most common task: given observed evidence, compute the posterior probability of a cause.

$$P(\text{Cause} \mid \text{Effect}) = \frac{P(\text{Effect} \mid \text{Cause})\; P(\text{Cause})}{P(\text{Effect})}$$

Worked Example: Given that "the grass is wet," what is more likely: rain or sprinkler?

- **贝叶斯网络（Bayesian Network）**： - 有向无环图（DAG）； - 节点 = 随机变量（如“是否下雨”、“是否洒水”、“草坪是否湿”）； - 边 = 条件依赖关系； - 每个节点配一个**条件概率表(CPT)**，如 $P(\text{WetGrass} \mid \text{Rain}, \text{Sprinkler})$。
$$P(\text{Rain} \mid \text{WetGrass}) = \frac{P(\text{WetGrass} \mid \text{Rain})\; P(\text{Rain})}{P(\text{WetGrass})}$$

Using the graph structure to decompose the joint probability and summing over hidden variables (e.g., Sprinkler, Cloudy), we can perform efficient inference even in complex networks.

13. Summary (本讲总结)

Core Takeaways (核心要点)

  1. Formal Logic (形式逻辑) provides the syntax and semantics for rigorous reasoning — the foundation of AI's ability to "think."
  2. Propositional Logic (命题逻辑) treats sentences as atomic truth values; it is simple but cannot express internal structure or relationships.
  3. First-Order Logic (一阶逻辑 / FOL) introduces individuals, predicates, and quantifiers, enabling the expression of why a conclusion follows — a major leap in expressiveness.
  4. The Four Laws of Logic (Identity, Non-Contradiction, Excluded Middle, Sufficient Reason) ensure consistency in reasoning systems.
  5. Curry–Howard Correspondence reveals a deep unity: propositions are types, proofs are programs, and logical reasoning is computation.
  6. Boolean Algebra & Digital Logic implement symbolic reasoning in hardware — from NAND gates to full adders to CPUs.
  7. Information Entropy provides a quantitative bridge between logic and learning: decision trees use information gain to split features, and FOIL uses the same principle to select predicates.
  8. Knowledge Graphs (知识图谱) operationalize logic into large-scale factual networks stored as triples \(\langle S, P, O \rangle\).
  9. FOIL learns explicit logical rules (Horn clauses) from knowledge graphs via sequential covering and information gain.
  10. Path Ranking Algorithm (PRA) uses graph paths as statistical features for relation prediction — a complementary, more scalable approach.
    11. 除了显式学逻辑规则外，还有一种更“统计学习式”的知识图谱关系推理方法：**路径排序算法（Path Ranking / Path Ranking Algorithm, PRA）**。
  11. Probabilistic Graphical Models (Bayesian Networks, Markov Networks) handle uncertain reasoning by combining graph structure with probability theory.
    12. 概率图模型（Probabilistic Graphical Models）把**图结构**与**概率论**结合，用来刻画这种不确定性。

The Big Picture (全景图)

This lecture traces two parallel trajectories:

Trajectory 1 — From Symbols to Circuits:
Formal Logic → Mathematical Logic → Digital Logic → Hardware (CPU)
"Making reasoning executable."

Trajectory 2 — From Facts to Inference:
Knowledge Graphs → FOIL / PRA → Probabilistic Graphical Models
"Making AI reason with both logic and uncertainty."

Together, they show that AI reasoning can be interpretable and symbolic (FOIL), statistical and scalable (PRA), or probabilistic and robust (PGMs) — and that all three rest on the logical and mathematical foundations laid out in this lecture.
